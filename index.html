Maven Discovery Playbook

For Any VP of CX, Customer Support, or Customer Experience Leader · B2B SaaS · Use Across All Discovery Calls

How to Use This

Every section follows the same flow: ASK the question, understand WHY, recognize WHAT YOU'LL HEAR from Gong data, then deliver a PRESCRIPTIVE BRIDGE that weaves Maven in. Show the slide. Drop the proof point. Always close back to resolution.

The Opening Hook

Before the First Question

Deliver this before you ask anything. It sets tone, shows pattern recognition, kills the pitch radar, and frames the next 45 minutes as a real conversation. Say it right after pleasantries.

Say This First
"Most CX leaders I talk to at companies your size are navigating the same tension right now. The business is asking for an AI strategy, the team has probably tried a few things that underdelivered, and there's some internal skepticism about whether this actually works at scale. I'm not here to pitch you today. I just want to understand where you actually are in that journey. What's working, what's not, what you've tried. Can we start there?"
Then pause. Let them respond. Do not follow this with a slide. Do not follow with a product feature. Let them talk first.
Why This Works

It uses "what I've seen" language that signals experience without arrogance. It validates their skepticism before they voice it. It signals you're there to understand, not close. And it opens the door for them to tell you about their prior AI attempts, which is your single most valuable piece of discovery information.

Core Mantras — Repeat These Throughout

Phrases That Should Come Out Naturally
  • "The goal isn't deflection — it's resolution. Those are fundamentally different outcomes." Deflection = customer gave up. Resolution = customer got what they needed. Say this whenever they use the word "deflection."
  • "It's not about L1/L2/L3 anymore — it's about access, not complexity." Use this when they try to categorize what AI can and can't handle.
  • "Bolt-on solutions hit a ceiling when it comes to actions." They can answer questions. But when a customer says "cancel my account" or "add 50 users" — they stop. Maven doesn't stop.
  • "What I've seen with companies like yours is..." Opens every proof point. Never say "most companies" — say "what I've seen."
  • "Crawl, walk, run — with the end state always being autonomous resolution." Use this when scoping. Grounds the conversation in a progression rather than a big-bang ask.
  • "Maven has three engines working together: Answer, Action, and Proactive." The reason this matters is each engine unlocks a different tier of resolution.
  • "One AI layer across your whole org — same knowledge, same guardrails, same logic — across every surface." This is the consistency argument.
  • "Turn your cost center into a growth center." Use this when the Proactive Engine comes up. This is when CX stops being defensive and starts driving revenue.

Crawl / Walk / Run Framework

How to Use This

Use this framework proactively throughout discovery and especially when scoping a next step. It keeps the conversation from feeling overwhelming, shows there's a clear path forward, and anchors the end state on resolution not just speed. Say: "We think about this in three phases..." and walk through it.

Phase 1 — Crawl
Answers
Connect your knowledge sources (KB, docs, website)
Deploy on one channel — chat or email first
Resolve the high-volume, high-repetition how-to questions
Target: 50%+ of volume resolved without a human
Time to value: weeks, not months
Lift required: minimal — no engineering heavy lifting
Phase 2 — Walk
Personalization
Connect CRM and user data (Salesforce, HubSpot, Gainsight)
Maven now knows who is asking and tailors every response
Tier-aware, account-aware, history-aware answers
Target: 70%+ resolution + higher CSAT
Lift required: CRM integration, some configuration
Segments: different experiences for different customer types
Phase 3 — Run
Actions + Proactive
Connect billing, product, and operational systems
Maven executes: subscriptions, refunds, seat changes, ticket creation
Proactive outreach based on usage signals and telemetry
Target: 90%+ resolution — autonomous, end-to-end
CX becomes a retention and revenue function, not just cost
This is the "growth center" moment
